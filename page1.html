About the Project

This digital humanities project argues for the rehumanization of those who have been executed in the state of Texas.

The Data

This project looks at the 553 death penalty executions that took place in Texas from 1982-2018. The dataset for this project comes from the Texas Department of Justice. It does not include the 12 executions that took place in the years of 2019-2020. Nor does it include the hundreds of executions that took place in Texas pre-1982, by means other than lethal injection.

The dataset provided by the Texas Department of Justice was further cleaned using OpenRefine. It included the following information: Date of Birth, Date of Offense, Highest Education Level, First Name, Last name, Age at Execution, Date Received, Execution Date, Race, County Executed, Eye Color, Weight, Height, Native County, Native State, Last Statement. Additional calculations were made using excel between the Date Received and Execution Date, as well as calculations between Date of Birth and Date of Offense. 

Presenting the Data 

The data visualizations, text analyses and maps for this project were created with Tableau Public and Voyant Online Tools. This website is hosted by github and was designed on Mobirise. 

Special attention was paid throughout this process to find ways to present this as a person-centered data story. These visualizations represent lives lost, not 'objective' data points. For this reason, visualization methods were selected that aimed to highlight the humanity and personhood of the individual who had been executed-- focusing in particular on biographical information and the executed's last words.
